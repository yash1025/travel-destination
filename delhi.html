About Delhi

Delhi, the capital city of India, is a dynamic and multifaceted metropolis that encapsulates the essence of the country's rich history and vibrant culture. With its sprawling urban landscape and a population exceeding 20 million, Delhi stands as one of the world's most populous cities. Its heritage is deeply rooted in centuries of political and cultural significance, tracing back to the times of the mighty Mughal Empire. One of Delhi's most iconic landmarks, the Red Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, symbolizes India's sovereignty and architectural grandeur. However, Delhi is more than just historical monuments; it's a diverse mosaic of cultures, languages, and traditions. This diversity is reflected in the city's culinary landscape, where tantalizing street food reigns supreme, from spicy chaats to mouthwatering kebabs.
